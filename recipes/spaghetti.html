Spaghetti

About This Recipe

This is one of my favorite recipes, found in the cookbook "Appeties" written by Anthony Bourdain. Don't be afraid of the anchovies, they add a deep umami flavor without being too fishy. This can be made using everyday ingredents found in your pantry. Don't skimp on the Parmigiano Reggiano.

Ingredents

  • 8 oil packed anchovy fillets, rinse, drained, and patted dry
  • 1 pound dry spaghetti
  • 1/4 cup olive oil (best availible, more to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 6 cloves of garlic thinly sliced
  • 1 cup flat leaf parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup Grated Parmigiano Reggiano (more to taste)

Steps

  1. Bring pot of salted water to boil, once boiling, cook pasta until al dente
  2. Warm oil in large saute pan over medium low
  3. Add garlic, pepper flakes, and anchovies to oil, cook slowly until anchovies evaporate. Do NOT brown garlic
  4. Add parsley to oil
  5. Add pasta straight from water to oil, increasing heat to medium (the water on the noodles will thicken the sauce)
  6. Add olive oil and pasta water as needed
  7. Salt to taste
  8. Transfer pasta to individual bowls and top with grated Parmigiano Reggiano
